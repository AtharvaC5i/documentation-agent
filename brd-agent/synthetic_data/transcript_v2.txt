MEETING TRANSCRIPT — Follow-Up Discovery Call #2
Project: ShopEase E-Commerce Platform
Client: RetailCorp India Pvt. Ltd.
Date: March 28, 2026
Duration: 35 minutes

Participants:
- Rajesh Mehta [Client PM]
- Sunita Agarwal [Client CEO]
- Vikram Patel [Client Head of IT]
- Arjun Shah [Our BA]
- Priya Nair [Our PM]

---

[00:01:12]
Arjun Shah: Good afternoon everyone. Following our first call, we've drafted the initial BRD and shared it with you. Today we want to go through your feedback and capture any changes.

Sunita Agarwal: Yes we reviewed it. Overall it looks good. But we have some important changes.

Rajesh Mehta: First thing — we discussed internally and we are NOT going with ZestMoney. That integration is cancelled. We only want Bajaj Finance for the no-cost EMI.

Arjun Shah: Understood, so ZestMoney is out of scope entirely?

Rajesh Mehta: Yes completely. Remove it.

[00:04:30]
Sunita Agarwal: Second thing — we want to add WhatsApp integration. Our customers use WhatsApp heavily. We want to send order confirmations, shipping updates, and promotional messages on WhatsApp in addition to SMS and email.

Arjun Shah: Is this using the WhatsApp Business API?

Sunita Agarwal: Yes. We already have a WhatsApp Business account. We need to integrate it.

Vikram Patel: We use Gupshup as our WhatsApp service provider. They have an API. I will share the documentation.

Priya Nair: Noted — WhatsApp via Gupshup API for transactional and promotional messages.

[00:09:15]
Rajesh Mehta: Third point — the mobile app. In the first call we said Android and iOS. We want to add a PWA also. Progressive Web App. Some of our customers in smaller cities have low-end phones and cannot install apps easily. PWA will serve them.

Arjun Shah: So three delivery channels now — Android native app, iOS native app, and PWA?

Rajesh Mehta: Correct.

Sunita Agarwal: The PWA should have the full purchase flow. Not a lite version.

[00:14:40]
Vikram Patel: I want to raise something about the SAP integration. In the previous discussion we said real-time inventory sync. But our SAP team has now told us that real-time webhooks from SAP are not possible with our current SAP version. We are on SAP ECC 6.0. The maximum we can do is every 15 minutes batch sync.

Arjun Shah: That is a significant change. So instead of real-time inventory, we have 15-minute polling?

Vikram Patel: Yes. And for product data, we said once a day before — that remains. But for inventory it is 15-minute batch, not real-time.

Priya Nair: This affects our low-stock and out-of-stock logic. We need to build a buffer.

Vikram Patel: Yes exactly. I suggest we maintain a 10% safety stock buffer in the platform. If SAP says 100 units, we show 90 on the website.

Arjun Shah: Good business rule — 10% buffer on displayed inventory.

[00:21:05]
Sunita Agarwal: We also want to change the return window. For electronics we said 7 days. We want to increase that to 10 days. Furniture stays at 30 days.

Rajesh Mehta: Also we want to add a replacement option not just refund. Some customers will prefer a replacement unit rather than money back.

Arjun Shah: So the return flow now has two options — refund to original payment method OR replacement of the same product?

Rajesh Mehta: Yes. And replacement should only be available if the product is in stock.

[00:26:30]
Vikram Patel: One more thing from the IT side. We need two-factor authentication for the admin panel. All admin users and store managers must use 2FA. Customers do not need it.

Arjun Shah: OTP-based 2FA?

Vikram Patel: Yes, mobile OTP. We don't want authenticator apps — our store managers are not tech-savvy enough.

Sunita Agarwal: For the executive dashboard on mobile — I want to be able to download reports as PDF from my phone. Right now the BRD just says view reports. I want PDF export.

[00:31:00]
Priya Nair: Any changes to the compliance requirements?

Rajesh Mehta: Yes actually. We are registering as a company in Singapore for international operations. So we now need to comply with Singapore PDPA in addition to Indian IT Act and GDPR for NRI customers.

Arjun Shah: Three compliance regimes — Indian IT Act, GDPR for NRI customers, and Singapore PDPA for international operations.

Sunita Agarwal: Correct.

Priya Nair: This also means some data may need to be stored in Singapore for those customers. The data residency requirement becomes more complex.

Vikram Patel: We will need to discuss this more. For now flag it as a decision pending.

[00:34:20]
Arjun Shah: Any other changes?

Rajesh Mehta: The vendor onboarding — in the first call we said vendors just upload products and admin approves. We now want a vendor KYC process. Vendors must submit GST certificate, PAN card, and bank account details before they can list products. This needs to be a formal onboarding workflow.

Sunita Agarwal: Yes vendor compliance is important. We cannot have unverified vendors on the platform.

Arjun Shah: Understood. Vendor KYC with document submission and admin verification workflow.

Priya Nair: Great. I think we have captured everything. We will update the BRD with these changes and share v2 within the week.

Sunita Agarwal: Thank you.

[END OF TRANSCRIPT]
